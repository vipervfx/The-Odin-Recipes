Spaghetti Recipe

Spaghetti Carbonara is a classic Italian pasta dish that’s creamy, comforting, and full of flavor. It combines simple ingredients—spaghetti, eggs, cheese, and crispy bacon or pancetta—into a rich, silky sauce without using cream. This dish is perfect for a quick weeknight dinner or impressing friends with authentic Italian flavors.

Ingredients

  • 200g (7 oz) spaghetti
  • 100g (3.5 oz) pancetta or bacon, diced
  • 2 large eggs
  • ½ cup grated Parmesan or Pecorino cheese
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced (optional)
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • A small handful of chopped parsley (optional, for garnish)

Instructions

  1. Cook the pasta: Boil spaghetti in salted water according to package instructions until al dente. Reserve ½ cup of pasta water before draining.
  2. Cook the pancetta/bacon: In a skillet over medium heat, cook the diced pancetta or bacon until crispy. If using garlic, add it in the last minute of cooking.
  3. Mix eggs and cheese: In a bowl, whisk together eggs and grated cheese until smooth.
  4. Combine everything: Add the drained pasta to the skillet with pancetta. Remove from heat, then quickly pour in the egg and cheese mixture, tossing rapidly to coat the pasta. Add a little reserved pasta water if it seems dry.
  5. Season and serve: Season with salt and plenty of black pepper. Garnish with parsley and extra cheese if desired. Serve immediately.
